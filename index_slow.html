Abstract

We present Dual3D, a novel text-to-3D generation framework that efficiently generates realistic 3D assets from textual descriptions in 1 minute. The key component of our method is a dual-mode multi-view latent diffusion model. One mode is for efficient 2D multi-view denoising, while the other is for 3D-consistent rendering-based denoising. The model is mostly tuned from a pre-trained 2D latent diffusion model by substituting the single-view latent denoising with a synchronized and interconnected denoising for both multi-view latents and tri-plane representations, circumventing the cost of training from scratch. With the proposed dual-mode toggling inference strategy to use different modes in different denoising steps during inference, our model is capable of generating a clean 3D neural surface in just 10 seconds. In order to mitigate the style bias inherent in the synthesized multi-view dataset, the texture map of the asset mesh, which is extracted from the generated neural surface by denoising, is further optimized by our efficient texture refinement to enhance the photo-realism and details in about 40 seconds.
